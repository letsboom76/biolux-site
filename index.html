General

About WPC Products

What is WPC (Wood-Plastic Composite)?

WPC stands for Wood-Plastic Composite, an engineered material made from a blend of wood fibers (typically 60%) and thermoplastic polymers (typically 35-40%). This creates a material that looks and feels like real wood but is significantly more durable, weather-resistant, and requires zero maintenance. It won't rot, warp, splinter, or need staining.

How does WPC compare to real wood?

WPC offers several advantages over traditional wood: no rotting, warping, or splintering; no need for staining, sealing, or painting; resistant to termites and pests; won't fade as quickly; easier to clean; and backed by a 25-year warranty. The only trade-off is that WPC doesn't have the exact same texture as natural wood grain, though our products come very close.

What colors are available?

Our standard color palette includes Teak (warm brown), Walnut (rich dark brown), Ashwood (light grey), and Charcoal (dark grey). Custom colors may be available for large orders. We recommend visiting our showroom to see samples in person, as colors can appear differently on screens.

Installation

Installation & DIY

Can I install BioLux products myself?

Yes! Our Super Kit fencing systems are specifically designed for DIY installation. Each kit comes with all necessary hardware, detailed instructions, and access to video tutorials. Most homeowners with basic tools can install a fence panel in about 30 minutes. For decking and larger projects, we recommend professional installation, which we also offer.

Do you offer professional installation?

Yes, we offer full-service professional installation throughout Southern California. Our installation team is factory-trained and follows strict quality standards. Professional installation also extends certain warranty protections. Contact us for a free installation quote.

What tools do I need for DIY installation?

For fence installation, you'll need: a drill/driver, level, tape measure, post hole digger (for in-ground posts), and concrete mix if setting posts. All specialty hardware and fasteners are included in our kits. For decking, additional tools like a circular saw, jigsaw, and chalk line are helpful.

Care & Maintenance

Cleaning & Upkeep

How do I clean WPC products?

Simple soap and water is usually all you need. For routine cleaning, just spray with a garden hose. For tougher stains, use a soft brush with mild dish soap and water. Avoid pressure washers above 1,500 PSI. Never use harsh chemicals, bleach, or abrasive cleaners as they can damage the surface.

Will WPC fade over time?

All outdoor materials experience some color change due to UV exposure and weathering. Our products are formulated to minimize fading, and any color change is covered under our fade warranty (up to 5 Delta E units). Most customers find any lightening to be subtle and natural-looking over the 25-year warranty period.

Do I need to seal or stain WPC?

No! One of the biggest advantages of WPC is that it never needs sealing, staining, or painting. The color is integrated throughout the material. This saves significant time and money compared to traditional wood, which typically needs re-staining every 2-3 years.

Ordering

Purchasing & Delivery

How do I calculate how much material I need?

For fencing, measure your total linear footage and divide by 6 (our standard panel width) to get the number of kits needed. For decking, calculate your square footage and add 10% for cuts and waste. We're happy to help with calculations — just call us with your measurements or send photos for a free estimate.

Do you deliver?

Yes, we offer delivery throughout Southern California. Delivery fees vary by distance and order size. Large orders may qualify for free delivery. You can also pick up from our Gardena showroom. We'll coordinate delivery timing with you to ensure someone is available to receive the materials.

Can I see samples before ordering?

Absolutely! We encourage visiting our Gardena showroom at 848 W 168th St to see full-size product displays and color samples in person. If you can't visit, we can mail small color samples for a nominal fee. Seeing the products in person is the best way to make your color selection.
